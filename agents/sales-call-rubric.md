# Sales-Call Scoring Rubric

The `sales-call-scorer` agent scores every transcript against these seven dimensions. Each is scored
**1–5**. Edit the weights to match how your team sells — the agent reads this file at score time.

| # | Dimension | What a 5 looks like | Weight |
|---|-----------|---------------------|--------|
| 1 | **Rapport & opening** | Warm, relevant opener; sets a clear agenda; earns the right to ask questions. | 10% |
| 2 | **Discovery / qualification** | Uncovers the real problem, impact, timeline and decision process with open questions and active listening. | 25% |
| 3 | **Value framing** | Ties the solution to the prospect's stated problem and quantified impact — not a feature dump. | 20% |
| 4 | **Objection handling** | Surfaces concerns, acknowledges them, responds with evidence, confirms it landed. | 15% |
| 5 | **Talk/listen ratio** | Rep talks roughly ≤45% of the time; prospect does most of the talking. | 10% |
| 6 | **Next-step / close** | Lands a specific, time-bound next step with the right people committed. | 15% |
| 7 | **Process & CRM hygiene** | Confirms budget/authority/need/timeline and captures what's needed to log the deal accurately. | 5% |

## Scoring

`weighted total = Σ (dimension score ÷ 5 × weight)` → a percentage.

**Bands**

| Band | Score | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| 🔴 Needs work | < 55% | Core gaps; book coaching before the next call of this type. |
| 🟡 Solid | 55–74% | Competent; one or two clear levers to lift. |
| 🟢 Strong | 75–89% | High quality; reinforce and replicate. |
| 🟢🟢 Exceptional | ≥ 90% | Model call; use as a coaching example. |

## Notes for the scorer
- Quote or paraphrase the specific moment behind each score.
- If the transcript can't support a dimension (e.g. call cut short), mark it `N/A` and renormalise
  the weights across the dimensions you could score.
- Always finish with the 2–3 highest-leverage coaching actions.
