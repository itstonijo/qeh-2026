## Week 1

**1. Educational — Trade Upgrade Program**

> A lot of employers ask me where they're going to find diesel mechanics for Heavy Mobile Plant. Here's one answer: RAEME and RAAF technicians.
>
> They've already spent years maintaining equipment under pressure, in the field, with zero tolerance for getting it wrong. Give them a Cert III and they're not learning a trade from scratch, they're translating one.
>
> That's the Trade Upgrade Program. It's not a one-off placement, it's a pipeline. If you're short on qualified operators, this is worth a conversation.

**2. STORY SLOT — PENDING REAL MATERIAL (do not fill with an invented story)**

> This slot was originally drafted with a fabricated placement story — no real candidate, no real employer, no real quote. Pulled per Toni Jo's "make it real" note.
>
> To fill this slot with a real story, we need from you: a specific placement you're comfortable featuring publicly, the outcome (in your own words, nothing invented), and whether the candidate/employer can be named or should be anonymised (e.g. "a veteran I placed into a mining role" with no identifying detail).
>
> **Important:** checking real records for this turned up sensitive personal material a candidate shared with you in confidence (background-check detail, personal history) — that kind of detail must never go into a public post, real or anonymised, without that person's explicit, informed consent to use it for this exact purpose. Pick someone and a story you know they'd be comfortable seeing on LinkedIn, and keep it to the professional outcome, not anything from their file.

**3. Objection-handling — why a veteran over a generalist**

> Get asked this a bit: why go through a veteran-specific recruiter instead of a general labour hire firm?
>
> Because we're not filling a body count. Every candidate's been vetted for the specific role, not just available for a start date. And every placement funds veteran support on the back end, since we're a certified social enterprise, not just a nice line in a pitch deck.
>
> If you're hiring for safety-critical trades and want people who show up with the work ethic already built in, that's where we're strongest.

## Week 2

**4. Awareness — the ADF-to-civilian skills gap**

> Mining and civil employers keep telling me the same thing: they can't fill safety-critical roles from the shrinking civilian pool.
>
> Meanwhile there are thousands of ADF members transitioning out every year with exactly the discipline, safety culture and hands-on technical skill those sites need. The gap isn't a talent gap, it's a translation gap. Nobody's connecting the two.
>
> That's the space we sit in.

**5. Story — why Ironside exists**

> Veteran-owned, Social Traders certified, and built by people who've actually worn the uniform.
>
> We say "we speak both languages, Defence and business" because it's true. Employers get someone who can explain what a candidate's ADF trade actually means on their site. Candidates get someone who won't make them re-explain their whole career from scratch.
>
> That's the model. Leading the transition, one placement at a time.

**6. Lead magnet — Trade Upgrade Program one-pager**

> If you're a RAEME or RAAF technician wondering what your trade looks like on civvy street, or an employer wondering where your next diesel mechanic is coming from, the Trade Upgrade Program is built for exactly that conversation.

> Send me a message and I'll send through what it looks like end to end.
