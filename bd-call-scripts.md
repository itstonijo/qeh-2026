# Ironside Resources — BD Call Scripts & Talking Points

Built 2026-06-27 to support the goal: win new paying clients. The emails open the door; the call wins the client. This is what to say when you get them on the phone (or in the room). Not a script to read word for word, prompts to keep you sharp. Targets match the top three in `bd-target-list.md`.

## The spine of every call (use for all three)
1. **Warm open, then get to it.** No long wind-up. "Thanks for taking five minutes."
2. **Name their pain in their words.** Show you know their world before you sell.
3. **One hook, not three.** Lead with the single thing that fits them. Hold the rest.
4. **Ask a question that gets a yes.** Get them talking about their need.
5. **Make the ask small.** A short next step (a snapshot, a 20-min meeting), not "give us your roles".
6. **Pin a next action before you hang up.** A date, a name, a thing you'll send.

## Reusable proof points (have these ready)
- 4,000+ vetted candidates, 200+ placed in mining and civil since 2018, 98% interview-to-hire.
- Veteran-owned, certified social enterprise (Social Traders).
- Trade Upgrade Program: RAEME and RAAF technicians into Cert III Heavy Mobile Plant, run as a pipeline.
- 3-month replacement guarantee on perm placements.
- Contractor option via CXC (payroll/compliance handled) for try-before-you-buy.

## Pricing, only when they ask (don't lead with it)
- Perm: 15% of TAR up to $120k, 17% above. 14/16% exclusive or retained. 3-month replacement guarantee.
- Contractor: 14% margin blue collar, 16% white collar, oncosts at actuals, 4-hr daily minimum, 14-day terms.
- Temp-to-perm: start on contract, reduced conversion fee the longer they're on hire.
- Hold the line: full candidate briefs come after terms are agreed. Before that, a snapshot only.

## 1. MADER GROUP — lead hook: Trade Upgrade pipeline
**Their world:** specialist heavy-mobile maintenance contractor, chasing HD/drill/digger fitters across the Pilbara and Bowen Basin, feeling the fitter shortage harder than most because supplying fitters *is* the business.

**Open:** "I know fitters are the hardest seat to fill right now, and you're carrying that demand constantly. That's actually why I called."

**Hook (say this):** "We run a Trade Upgrade Program that takes ex-Army and Air Force technicians and puts them through Cert III Heavy Mobile Plant. It's built as a pipeline, so instead of fighting the market every time a seat opens, you'd have a steady feed of work-ready fitters who already know how to maintain gear under pressure."

**Question that gets a yes:** "If I could give you a predictable trickle of fitters rather than a scramble each time, would that be worth looking at?"

**The ask:** "Let me send you a snapshot of two we've got ready now. If the calibre's right, we talk volume."

**Objections:**
- *"We use our own pipeline / labour hire already."* → "Good, this isn't replacing it, it's a veteran feed on top, for the seats your current channels can't fill fast enough. You only pay when one lands and stays."
- *"Are they actually ticketed?"* → "Cert III Heavy Mobile Plant, and I verify every one personally before they reach you. Plus a 3-month replacement guarantee on perms."
- *"Rates?"* → give contractor margins, note oncosts at actuals, 14-day terms.

## 2. WESTRAC — lead hook: Trade Upgrade pipeline + apprentice pathway
**Their world:** Cat dealer, service business runs on diesel fitters and a steady apprentice intake; the shortage hits their workshops directly.

**Open:** "Your service side lives and dies on diesel fitters, and they're getting harder to find. I think we can feed both your qualified roles and your apprentice intake."

**Hook (say this):** "We move ex-Defence technicians, RAEME and RAAF mechanical backgrounds, into Cert III Heavy Mobile Plant. They take to Cat gear fast and the discipline's already baked in. Because it runs as a pipeline, you can plan your intake around it instead of reacting to each vacancy."

**Question that gets a yes:** "Would it help to have a veteran feed sitting behind both your fitter roles and your apprentice program?"

**The ask:** "Give me 15 minutes and a couple of snapshots, and you can judge the calibre yourself."

**Objections:**
- *"We run our own apprentice scheme."* → "Perfect fit then. Veterans come in older, work-ready and reliable, they lift completion rates on schemes like yours rather than competing with them."
- *"We're a big shop, why a specialist agency?"* → "Because we only do this lane. Veteran-owned, and the social-enterprise credit is a bonus your ESG team will like."
- *"Cost?"* → perm or contractor depending on the role; mention replacement guarantee.

## 3. NORTH EAST LINK / SPARK — lead hook: social procurement door
**Their world:** tier-1 consortium, 8,000 tunnel jobs, recruiting hard across engineering, commercial, site and trades, and carrying Victorian social procurement and local-jobs reporting targets.

**Open:** "With the tunnels ramping, you're resourcing flat out. I called because we can help you fill roles and hit a reporting target with the same hire."

**Hook (say this):** "We're veteran-owned and certified by Social Traders. Under Victoria's Social Procurement Framework, spend with us counts toward the project's social procurement commitments. So an ADF veteran placement fills the seat and ticks the box at once. Our people come out of Defence work-ready, many security-clearable, and used to safety-critical sites."

**Question that gets a yes:** "Is hitting those social procurement numbers something the project's actively tracking right now?"

**The ask:** "Who holds the social procurement and local-jobs side? I'd value 20 minutes with them, and I'll come to you."

**Objections:**
- *"We have preferred-supplier panels."* → "Understood. Two ways in: get us on the panel via the social-enterprise route, or start with a single specialist package where the veteran-plus-procurement angle is hard to beat."
- *"Does Social Traders spend really count?"* → "Yes. The Victorian framework defines a Victorian social enterprise as one certified by Social Traders. I can send the reference." (Confirm Ironside's VIC premises before leaning hard on the 'Victorian' framing.)
- *"Why veterans for project roles?"* → "Work-ready, security-clearable, no visa lag versus the offshore hires tier-1s are flying in, and they thrive in safety-critical, structured environments."
