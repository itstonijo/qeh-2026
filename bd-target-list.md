## How to read this
Each target has: **why now** (the live trigger), **likely roles** (what they're resourcing), **the Ironside angle** (the hook only we can use), **ICP fit**, and **contact path**. Targets are grouped by how warm/winnable they are, hottest first.

## The two hooks that cut through this quarter
1. **Trade Upgrade Program ↔ the heavy diesel fitter shortage.** The market needs ~2,700 new heavy diesel fitters and ~8,110 plant operators by 2030, and the experienced-fitter pool can't fill it (fill rates on technical mining roles sit around 42%). RAEME/RAAF technicians into Cert III Heavy Mobile Plant is a *pipeline* answer to a structural shortage, not a one-off req. This is the sharpest thing we own. Lead with it for anyone running heavy mobile fleet.
2. **Veteran-owned + Social Traders certified ↔ Victoria's social procurement obligations.** Every Big Build project (North East Link, Marinus Link, the transmission build) carries social procurement and local-jobs targets. Spending with a Social Traders certified social enterprise counts toward those targets. We let a tier-1 hit a reporting KPI *and* fill a safety-critical role with the same placement. No competitor in our lane (ERS, WithYouWithMe, the NFPs, WorkPac/Programmed) can offer both at once.

## TIER A — hottest, chase first

### 1. Mader Group (heavy mobile plant maintenance contractor)
- **Why now:** Actively recruiting HD Fitters, Drill Fitters and Digger Fitters across the Pilbara and Bowen Basin in 2026, on rotational rosters. A specialist maintenance contractor whose entire model is supplying fitters — they feel the shortage harder than anyone.
- **Likely roles:** Heavy diesel fitters, digger/drill fitters, field service techs. $110k-$165k+ packages — well clear of the $80k floor.
- **The Ironside angle:** Trade Upgrade Program is a bullseye. We don't just fill the current vacancy, we hand them a repeatable RAEME/RAAF → Cert III diesel mechanic pipeline. Plus contractor workforce via CXC if they want try-before-you-buy.
- **ICP fit:** Strong. Specialised labour hire in a specialised field — exactly the lane we chase, not body-shop volume.
- **Contact path:** Listed company, talent acquisition team is reachable. Warm email lead with the Trade Upgrade pipeline. LinkedIn to find the TA/resourcing manager (manual).

### 2. WesTrac (Caterpillar dealer — heavy equipment sales, service, parts)
- **Why now:** Already a named target. Cat's WA/NSW dealer runs a huge HD fitter and apprentice demand curve; the diesel fitter shortage hits their service business directly.
- **Likely roles:** Heavy diesel fitters/mechanics, field service, apprentices, branch supervisors.
- **The Ironside angle:** Trade Upgrade Program again — veterans with RAEME/RAAF mechanical backgrounds slot straight into Cat servicing. Veteran work ethic + a structured pipeline they can plan around.
- **ICP fit:** Strong. Technical, safety-critical, above the pay floor.
- **Contact path:** Warm email to talent/early-careers team. Reference their apprentice intake.

### 3. North East Link — Spark Consortium (Webuild, CPB Contractors, GS E&C, China Construction Oceania; Ventia for O&M)
- **Why now:** 8,000 jobs on the tunnels alone, largest road investment in Victoria's history, actively recruiting across engineering, commercial, admin and trades through 2026.
- **Likely roles:** Site engineers, project/contract admin, WHS, supervisors, site admin, plus trades and operators.
- **The Ironside angle:** The social procurement hook leads here. Victorian project, Victorian law (our terms already sit there). Veteran-owned + Social Traders certified helps Spark evidence its social procurement and local-jobs commitments while filling technical roles. "We speak both languages, Defence and business" lands well with a safety-and-compliance-heavy program.
- **ICP fit:** Strong — tier-1 project supply, our sweet spot. Caveat: tier-1s often run preferred-supplier panels, so the realistic entry is a specific package, a subcontractor, or the social-procurement door rather than the head contract.
- **Contact path:** Two routes — (a) the project's inclusion/social procurement lead (the door competitors can't use), (b) subcontractors and specialist package holders down the supply chain. Start with the inclusion angle, it's differentiated.

### 4. Marinus Link (Vic–Tas interconnector; Gippsland preparatory works underway)
- **Why now:** Preparatory works started across Gippsland in 2026, lead contractors being appointed, actively courting local suppliers and workforce. Early enough that a supplier relationship now compounds as packages ramp.
- **Likely roles:** Civil trades, operators, site admin, WHS, supervisors — growing as construction scales.
- **The Ironside angle:** Regional social-licence + local/veteran workforce story is exactly what a transmission project under community scrutiny wants to tell. Social Traders certification gives them a procurement line to point to.
- **ICP fit:** Strong, civil construction in the ICP, regional Victoria.
- **Contact path:** They publish a workforce/supplier engagement channel (marinuslink.com.au/workforce). Register interest, then warm email the workforce/local-content lead.
