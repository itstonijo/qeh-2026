# Ironside Resources — Competitor Analysis

Prepared 2026-06-20. Veteran recruitment and labour hire into mining, civil, defence and logistics (Australia).

**Method and honesty note.** CLAUDE.md had no confirmed competitors, so the set below was identified by research, not given. Findings come from web search plus attempted page fetches; most competitor sites (kinexus.com.au, withyouwithme.com, ers, troocoo, most .gov.au/.org.au) returned HTTP 403 to automated fetchers, so a lot rests on search-result snippets with every claim URL-cited. Anything unconfirmed is marked `[unverified]` or "not found." No pricing percentages are published by any competitor; assume the standard model unless cited.

The competitor field falls into four buckets:
1. Specialist veteran/defence recruiters — **Kinexus**, **ERS Recruitment**, **Boots2Business**
2. Venture-backed veteran tech reskiller — **WithYouWithMe (WYWM)**
3. Free not-for-profit / government programs — **RSL Employment**, **Working Spirit**, **Soldier On**, **federal & NSW Veteran Employment Programs**
4. Generalist mining/civil labour hire that wins the same roles without the veteran angle — **WorkPac**, **Programmed**

## PART 1 — WHO THEY ARE

### Kinexus (formerly Kinetic Recruitment) — defence-specialist recruiter
- **Sells:** full-lifecycle permanent + contract recruitment, project recruitment / RPO, workforce planning/mapping, hiring-manager interview training, and security-clearance sponsorship (DISP member, sponsors to NV2). Pricing not published. ([employers](https://www.kinexus.com.au/how-we-help/employers), [FAQ](https://www.kinexus.com.au/faq), [ICT](https://www.kinexus.com.au/ict))
- **Targets:** Commonwealth/Defence agencies, primes and defence SMEs; candidates are technical white-collar — engineering, ICT/cyber, PM, ILS, security-cleared. ([disciplines/defence](https://www.kinexus.com.au/disciplines/defence))
- **Positioning:** "Australia's first defence-specialist recruitment agency," 25 years, heritage + clearance fluency + lived military experience. ([about](https://www.kinexus.com.au/about))
- **Content:** monthly defence-workforce blog; flagship "Defence Industry Insights" salary survey, now 12th edition, picked up by trade press; LinkedIn ~21k followers. ([blog](https://www.kinexus.com.au/blog), [DII microsite](https://kx.kinexus.com.au/), [ADM coverage](https://www.australiandefence.com.au/business/new-defence-industry-insights-report-from-kinexus), [LinkedIn](https://au.linkedin.com/company/kinexus-australia))
- **Strengths:** Glassdoor 4.0/5 (~16 reviews, 79% recommend); documented case study filling 25 engineering/ILS roles in ~5 months; recognised thought leader. ([Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com.au/Reviews/Kinexus-Reviews-E842923.htm), [case studies](https://www.kinexus.com.au/how-we-help/employers/case-studies))
- **Weaknesses:** Glassdoor gripes on low pay/progression, one alleging nepotism; comp is the lowest sub-score (3.4). No mining/civil/trades/labour-hire offering found. ([Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com.au/Reviews/Kinexus-Reviews-E842923.htm))
- **Recent moves:** June 2026 hiring (DISP Security Officer tied to a client "DISP uplift"; internal recruitment consultant in Melbourne); DII 12th edition (Apr 2026); new SA office staffed with veterans. ([job](https://www.kinexus.com.au/job/disp-security-officer-671947), [job](https://www.kinexus.com.au/job/recruitment-consultant-671446), [Defence Connect](https://www.defenceconnect.com.au/key-enablers/8899-kinexus-employs-adf-veterans-at-new-sa-office))
- **Verdict:** veteran-owned, but a different lane — defence-industry white collar and clearances, not mining/civil trades.

### WithYouWithMe (WYWM) — veteran tech reskiller

([startupdaily](https://www.startupdaily.net/advice/withyouwithme-skills-training-job-search-veterans/), [itbrief — split](https://itbrief.com.au/story/withyouwithme-spins-out-greenbeam-as-global-saas-firm), [cio](https://www.cio.com/article/206022/withyouwithme-raises-5m-software-development-effort-enters-hypergrowth.html))
- **Targets:** veterans first, plus spouses, neurodivergent, women in tech, Indigenous, youth; buyers are large enterprise/gov/defence (BHP, Endeavour, BAE, Leonardo, Aus Gov/Defence). ([veteransemployment.gov.au](https://www.veteransemployment.gov.au/winners-finalists/withyouwithme-0), [itbrief](https://itbrief.com.au/story/withyouwithme-spins-out-greenbeam-as-global-saas-firm))
- **Positioning:** "Potential, not experience" / skills-based hiring; veteran-founded mission to end underemployment. ([withyouwithme.com](https://withyouwithme.com/))
- **Content:** blog, case-study hub, in-the-news page, active LinkedIn; cadence `[unverified]`. ([blog](https://withyouwithme.com/blog/), [LinkedIn](https://au.linkedin.com/company/with-you-with-me))
- **Strengths:** strong enterprise/gov logos; claims 20,000+ veterans trained; Deloitte Tech Fast 50 history. ([veteransemployment.gov.au](https://www.veteransemployment.gov.au/winners-finalists/withyouwithme-0))
- **Weaknesses:** Glassdoor ~3.4/5 (132 reviews) with repeated toxic-culture, long-hours, layoffs and broken-pay complaints; Trustpilot ~2.3/5 (~11) with candidates ignored after training; Jan 2026 split + CEO change signal cost/strategic pressure. ([Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com/Reviews/WithYouWithMe-Reviews-E2984753.htm), [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/withyouwithme.com))
- **Recent moves:** company split (Jan 2026), new CEOs (Larter at WYWM, Kouparitsas at Greenbeam), Greenbeam–GED partnership. ([itbrief](https://itbrief.com.au/story/withyouwithme-spins-out-greenbeam-as-global-saas-firm), [withyouwithme media](https://withyouwithme.com/media/company-separation-and-new-saas-launch/))
- **Verdict:** tech/cyber/data, not mining/civil/trades. Competes for the veteran talent pool and the veteran brand, currently distracted and reputationally bruised.

### ERS Recruitment (Executive Risk Solutions) — veteran-owned, the closest head-to-head
- **Sells:** ex-Defence placement, spousal placement, permanent/contract/temp, executive search, CV help; IncubatERS (veteran business transition). Pricing not found. ([services](https://www.ersrecruitment.com.au/services), [IncubatERS](https://www.ersrecruitment.com.au/incubaters))
- **Targets:** ex-ADF and transitioning members (~5,000 cited); employers in government, defence, multinationals and **mining, oil & gas**. ([VEP listing](https://www.vep.veterans.nsw.gov.au/jobseekers/further-support/recruitment/), [ERS](http://www.executiverisksolutions.com.au/services/ers-recruitment/))
- **Positioning:** pathways for ex-Defence personnel; led by Special Forces / emergency-services / law-enforcement team; high success in **security and emergency-management** roles. Founder ex-SAS Scott Houston. ([ERS](http://www.executiverisksolutions.com.au/services/ers-recruitment/))
- **Content:** thin/dated — most-cited blog post is from 2017; Facebook for promos; parent ERS more active on LinkedIn. ([2017 post](https://www.ersrecruitment.com.au/single-post/2017/05/02/What-are-the-main-issues-facing-Ex-Defence-personnel-when-transitioning-into-a-civilian-life), [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/executive-risk-solutions))
- **Strengths:** genuinely veteran-owned; won WA Employer Support Award; parent has 110+ staff across Perth/Brisbane/Malaysia/Singapore and a deep mining/oil-and-gas client base. ([WA award](https://www.executiverisksolutions.com.au/wa-employer-support-award/))
- **Weaknesses:** recruitment brand subordinated to the security business; dated web presence; no public reviews/case studies for the recruitment arm. ([2017 post](https://www.ersrecruitment.com.au/single-post/2017/05/02/What-are-the-main-issues-facing-Ex-Defence-personnel-when-transitioning-into-a-civilian-life))
- **Verdict:** the single most direct rival — veteran-owned, mining/oil-and-gas — but WA-centric and skewed to security/emergency rather than trades, and not a certified social enterprise.

### Boots2Business — partnership, not a standalone recruiter
- **Sells:** veteran placement via **Troocoo** (recruiter, Brisbane) paired with **Go2Health** (veteran health clinic). Pricing not found. ([VEP listing](https://www.vep.veterans.nsw.gov.au/jobseekers/further-support/recruitment/), [Troocoo veteran page](https://www.troocoo.com/people-advisory/veteran-employment))
- **Targets:** employers wanting veteran talent; candidates overlap Go2Health's 7,000+ veteran patients. ([Go2Health](https://www.go2health.com.au/veteran-care/))
- **Strengths:** backed by an established multi-discipline recruiter + a unique veteran-health partner. **Weaknesses:** thin standalone brand, search-name collision with the US SBA program, veteran-ownership `[unverified]`, recent activity not confirmable, not mining/trades-specialist. ([SBA program](https://www.sba.gov/sba-learning-platform/boots-business))
- **Verdict:** low-intensity competitor; broad/white-collar, initiative-grade brand.

### Free programs (RSL Employment, Working Spirit, Soldier On, federal & NSW VEP)
- **Model:** all free to employers, none are fee-for-service recruiters with commercial accountability — job boards, pledge networks, recognition schemes and career coaching, funded by grants/charity. ([veteransemployment.gov.au](https://www.veteransemployment.gov.au/employers-ready-hire-veterans/connecting-veterans))
- **RSL Employment Program:** free coaching + employer network + jobs board; ~1,300 placed since 2018; NSW expansion via RSL LifeCare (Dec 2024). ([RSL Australia](https://www.rslaustralia.org/employment), [RSL Qld](https://rslqld.org/news/rsl-lifecare-partners-with-rsl-queensland))
- **Working Spirit (WA):** TEP job-matching portal, free to employers; ~180 placed since 2016, run by ~1 FTE + volunteers; grant-dependent. ([TEP](https://www.workingspirit.org.au/employers-tep), [LinkedIn](https://au.linkedin.com/company/working-spirit-ltd))
- **Soldier On:** Pathways coaching + Pledge Companies + jobs board; 13,100+ participants but only ~74 employment placements last year — thin conversion. ([Soldier On](https://soldieron.org.au/supporting-you/employment/))
- **Federal VEP (DVA):** promotion, free job advertising, Veteran Employment Commitment badge tiers; **funded only to 31 Dec 2026 on a $0.5M six-month extension** — not a recruiter, sources no candidates. ([about VEP](https://www.veteransemployment.gov.au/about/veteran-employment-program), [budget sheet](https://www.dva.gov.au/sites/default/files/2026-05/budget-2026-27-info-sheet-continuing-support.pdf))
- **NSW VEP:** education/awareness platform; ~4,000 into NSW Government since 2016, but **NSW public sector only**, not private employers. ([NSW VEP](https://www.vep.veterans.nsw.gov.au/about), [Report 5](https://www.vep.veterans.nsw.gov.au/assets/veterans-affairs/NSW-Veterans-Employment-Program-Report-5.pdf))
- **Verdict:** strong on candidate supply/awareness and on price (free), weak as a substitute for an active, accountable, guaranteed placement. They erode the "we help veterans" emotional pitch but don't deliver a vetted shortlist to a specific brief.

### Generalist mining/civil labour hire (WorkPac, Programmed) — where the actual deals leak
- **WorkPac:** Australia's largest privately owned labour-hire/recruitment firm; explicit mining/resources specialisation, 35+ locations, ~6,500 field workers, ~$1.07bn revenue (aggregator figure). Wins on scale, regional footprint, compliance support. Worker reviews ~3.2/5: low pay, no continuity, weak duty-of-care. No veteran program found. ([workpac.com](https://www.workpac.com/), [mining](https://www.workpac.com/staff/blue-collar/mining/), [Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.co.in/Reviews/WorkPac-Reviews-E657314.htm), [Indeed](https://au.indeed.com/cmp/Workpac/reviews))
- **Programmed (PERSOL group):** dedicated Mining & Resources staffing; claims largest contingent labour pool in Australia, ~3.5M mining labour hours/year; bundles maintenance/asset management. Worker reviews ~3.5/5: pay errors, cancelled shifts, blacklisting threats. A "VETS" veteran label appeared once but the page 403'd — `[unverified]`. ([Programmed mining](https://skilled.programmed.com.au/service/staffing-services/mining-resources/), [Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com.au/Reviews/Programmed-Australia-Reviews-E652644.htm))
- **Verdict:** they out-scale Ironside enormously and compete on price/speed without the veteran angle. Their documented weakness is transactional worker treatment — exactly what a retention-led veteran specialist can position against.

## PART 2 — THE GAPS (where Ironside wins)

- **Kinexus** promises defence-workforce mastery but **ignores mining/civil trades and blue-collar labour hire entirely** — a lane Ironside owns. Messaging angle they can't credibly use: "veteran tradies and mobile-plant operators on site." Their content avoids mining ROI.
- **WYWM** promises veteran opportunity but under-delivers on follow-through (Trustpilot 2.3, "ignored after training") and is **tech-only**; civil/trades is white space. Their current split/turmoil is a window. They can't credibly claim mining-trades depth or hands-on, low-volume, high-touch placement.
- **ERS** has the veteran-owned + mining credential but is **WA-centric, security/emergency-skewed, dormant on content, and not a social enterprise**. Ironside can out-publish them trivially and own the eastern-states + Social Traders/ESG angle.
- **Boots2Business** is barely a brand — easy to out-position on a clear, veteran-owned, trades-focused identity.
- **Free programs** can't offer a vetted shortlist, speed, a guarantee, or single-owner accountability. The segment they ignore: employers who need a specific safety-critical hire **now**, with recourse if it fails. Ironside's 3-month replacement guarantee is precisely the gap.
- **WorkPac/Programmed** under-deliver on worker care and continuity (well-documented). The angle they cannot use: "we know your veteran worker, we keep them, and every placement is reportable social-procurement spend." They avoid the veteran-ESG story entirely.

## PART 4 — THE PLAYBOOK

1. **#1 positioning move this month:** Plant a flag nobody else holds — "Australia's veteran-owned specialist for safety-critical **trades and mobile-plant operators** into mining and civil, placed with a 3-month replacement guarantee and reportable as social-procurement spend." (Veteran-owned + trades + commercial accountability + ESG — no competitor combines all four.)
2. **Content gap to fill first:** Mining-trades outcome stories on **LinkedIn**, as a recurring case-study format — the diesel-mechanic / Heavy Mobile Plant Trade Upgrade pathway (illustrative examples only: a North East Link-style placement, Fortescue/Pilbara mechanics — not real cases, real stories need to come from Toni Jo, see automations/linkedin-personal-brand.md). Kinexus does defence salary surveys, WYWM does tech, ERS does nothing, free programs do coaching — **no one owns "veteran tradie ROI on a mine/civil site."** One candidate-outcome post + one short data post per week.
3. **Segment none of them serve well:** mid-tier mining/civil contractors and tier-1 subcontractors who need **a handful of vetted safety-critical trades/operators, fast, with veteran-ESG credit** — too small for WorkPac/Programmed's enterprise machine, out of scope for Kinexus (defence) and WYWM (tech), and unreachable through free job boards. The Trade Upgrade pipeline is the wedge.
4. **STOP doing:** chasing generalist, high-volume, commodity blue-collar labour hire. WorkPac and Programmed do it cheaper at scale and the worker-care risk is theirs to absorb. CLAUDE.md already says "specialised fields only" — hold that line and don't get dragged into body-shop reqs you can't win on price.
5. **Unfair advantage (hardest to copy):** the stack of **genuine veteran ownership + Social Traders certification (verified, reportable social-procurement/ESG spend) + the niche Trade Upgrade Program pipeline + a commercial guarantee.** A generalist can't fake veteran-owned; a free program can't offer accountability; Kinexus/WYWM can't credibly claim mining-trades depth; ERS lacks the social-enterprise certification. The certification + authenticity + trades-pipeline combination is the moat.

## Confidence & limitations
- High confidence: the four-bucket competitive structure; each competitor's core model, focus and review signals; the WYWM Jan-2026 split; the federal VEP funding cliff (31 Dec 2026); ERS being the closest veteran-owned mining rival.
- Medium confidence: scale/revenue/placement figures (aggregator and snippet-sourced); free-program placement counts.
- Low/unverified: any competitor pricing (none published); a Programmed veteran ("VETS") program; Boots2Business ownership; Trojan veteran status. All flagged inline.
- Most target sites blocked automated fetching (HTTP 403), so verbatim homepage/hero copy and blog cadence lean on search snippets. A manual browser pass on kinexus.com.au, withyouwithme.com, ersrecruitment.com.au and the Programmed staffing pages would firm these up.
